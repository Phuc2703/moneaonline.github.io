This blog serves as my public notebook where I share:

My Testing Methodology

To provide genuinely useful information, I've developed a rigorous testing framework that goes beyond surface-level reviews

1

Minimum 30-Day Tests

Every method gets at least one month of dedicated testing before I form conclusions. Many "overnight success" claims fall apart under sustained effort.

2

Public Tracking Sheets

My Google Sheets track exact time invested, money earned, and key metrics. Nothing hidden - you see the raw numbers behind my results.

3

Beginner-First Approach

I prioritize methods that don't require large upfront investments or existing audiences, focusing on what's truly accessible to newcomers.

4

Failure Documentation

When something doesn't work, I analyze why and share those lessons. Understanding pitfalls is more valuable than just seeing successes.

5

Tool Comparisons

I test competing tools/services side-by-side to identify which deliver the best value for beginners versus advanced users.

6

Community Feedback

Reader experiences shape my testing priorities. Your real-world results help improve the methodology over time.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why should I trust your recommendations?

I don't expect blind trust - that's why I provide complete transparency. You'll see my actual testing data, including screenshots of earnings, time tracking, and both successful and failed experiments. I'm documenting the real journey, not just the highlights.

How much time does this really require?

From my testing, plan for at least 10-15 hours per week to see meaningful results. Some methods require more upfront time that decreases later (like content creation), while others need consistent weekly effort (like survey platforms). I provide time estimates for each method I review.

What's your affiliate disclosure policy?

Every affiliate link is clearly marked. I never recommend products I haven't personally used for at least 30 days. If I receive compensation for a review, it's prominently disclosed. My priority is maintaining trust through complete transparency.

How do you choose what to test next?

Reader suggestions and industry trends guide my testing roadmap. I prioritize methods that: 1) Are accessible to beginners, 2) Don't require large investments, and 3) Have realistic earning potential based on my research.
